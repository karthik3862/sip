Mathru Poorna Scheme

Scheme Details

The Mathru Poorna Scheme is an initiative by the Karnataka government to provide nutritious meals to pregnant and lactating women in rural areas. The scheme aims to improve maternal health and reduce malnutrition and anemia among women and newborns.

Benefits

  • Nutritious meals served daily to pregnant and lactating women in Anganwadi centers.
  • Meals include rice, dal, vegetables, eggs, and milk to ensure balanced nutrition.
  • Reduction in anemia and improvement in maternal health.
  • Better health outcomes for newborns through improved maternal nutrition.
  • Targeted assistance for women in rural areas to address food insecurity.

Eligibility

  • Pregnant women residing in Karnataka, particularly in rural areas.
  • Lactating mothers up to six months post-delivery.
  • Women registered at the nearest Anganwadi center.
  • Women who are part of Below Poverty Line (BPL) families or economically disadvantaged.

Application Process

  • Visit the nearest Anganwadi center to register for the scheme.
  • Submit proof of pregnancy or postnatal status (medical certificate).
  • Provide necessary identification and proof of residence.
  • Regularly visit the Anganwadi center to avail the meal services.
  • Participate in health check-ups and counseling sessions offered under the scheme.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Medical certificate confirming pregnancy or lactation status.
  • Proof of residence (e.g., ration card, utility bill, etc.).
  • BPL certificate (if applicable).
  • Photographs for registration.
