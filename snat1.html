National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE)

Scheme Details

The National Programme for Health Care of the Elderly (NPHCE) is a government initiative aimed at providing dedicated healthcare services to the elderly population in India. The program focuses on improving access to healthcare services, enhancing elderly care infrastructure, and promoting awareness about geriatric health issues.

Benefits

  • Specialized healthcare services for elderly individuals, including geriatric care and rehabilitation.
  • Free or subsidized medical treatment for senior citizens in government hospitals.
  • Provision of long-term care services, such as palliative care and physiotherapy.
  • Increased accessibility to geriatric healthcare professionals in both urban and rural areas.

Eligibility

  • Indian citizens aged 60 years or above.
  • Senior citizens who require specialized geriatric care, rehabilitation, or medical treatment.
  • Residents of India (both urban and rural areas are eligible for coverage).

Application Process

  • Visit a government healthcare facility or empaneled hospital offering NPHCE services.
  • Check eligibility by submitting necessary documents such as age proof and medical history.
  • If eligible, register for the program at the hospital's registration counter.
  • Upon registration, receive the necessary healthcare services, including medical consultations, treatments, and follow-up care.

Documents Required

  • Aadhaar card or other government-issued ID for identity verification.
  • Proof of age (e.g., Birth certificate, Passport, or any government-issued ID showing the date of birth).
  • Medical records or prescriptions (if seeking specific treatment).
  • Residential proof (e.g., Ration card, utility bill, or voter ID).
