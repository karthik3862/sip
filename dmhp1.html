District Mental Health Programme

Programme Details

The District Mental Health Programme (DMHP) is an initiative by the Government of India aimed at providing mental health services to the people of India at the district level. The programme focuses on providing mental health care to individuals with mental health conditions and promoting mental well-being across the community.

Benefits

  • Access to mental health services at the district level.
  • Screening and early detection of mental health disorders.
  • Provision of mental health care for those with conditions like depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, and other psychiatric disorders.
  • Awareness programs to reduce stigma associated with mental health issues.
  • Collaboration with local healthcare providers for continuous care and follow-up.

Eligibility

  • Must be a resident of the district where the programme is implemented.
  • Individuals suffering from mental health conditions such as depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, etc.
  • Must be seeking assistance for mental health care through the DMHP network.

Application Process

  • Visit the nearest district hospital or a participating healthcare center offering DMHP services.
  • Fill out the application form for mental health support under the District Mental Health Programme.
  • Submit necessary documents like medical reports or diagnosis related to mental health conditions.
  • Once registered, the healthcare center will provide counseling and treatment options, including follow-up care.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Ration card, etc.).
  • Proof of residency in the district (e.g., utility bills, ration card).
  • Medical reports or diagnosis of mental health conditions (if applicable).
  • Referral letter from a primary healthcare provider (if required).
  • Passport-sized photograph of the applicant.
