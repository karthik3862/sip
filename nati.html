National Kidney Health Program

Scheme Details

The National Kidney Health Program is a government initiative aimed at improving kidney health awareness and providing treatment for individuals suffering from kidney diseases, especially those with a higher risk of developing kidney-related complications. This program is aimed at providing education, screening, and free or subsidized treatment for kidney disease patients.

Benefits

  • Early detection and screening for kidney diseases.
  • Free or subsidized treatment for kidney diseases.
  • Special care for at-risk populations including those with diabetes, hypertension, and other kidney-related risk factors.
  • Access to dialysis and kidney transplant services for eligible patients.
  • Educational campaigns to promote kidney health and prevention of kidney diseases.

Eligibility

  • Must be an Indian citizen.
  • Individuals diagnosed with kidney diseases or those at high risk (e.g., patients with hypertension or diabetes).
  • Patients from economically disadvantaged backgrounds are prioritized for financial assistance.
  • Must be registered with a participating hospital or healthcare center.

Application Process

  • Visit the nearest government hospital or any participating healthcare center.
  • Fill out the application form available at the hospital or online.
  • Submit the necessary medical documentation, including medical history and diagnosis.
  • Once registered, eligible patients will be assessed for treatment, including dialysis or kidney transplant, if needed.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, or Ration card).
  • Income certificate (if applicable).
  • Medical diagnosis report from a recognized hospital.
  • Blood tests and kidney function reports.
  • Recent passport-sized photograph.
