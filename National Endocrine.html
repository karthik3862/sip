National Endocrine Health Scheme

Scheme Details

The National Endocrine Health Scheme aims to improve the diagnosis, treatment, and management of endocrine disorders in India. The scheme focuses on providing specialized care for conditions such as diabetes, thyroid disorders, and other hormonal imbalances through government healthcare services and financial assistance for treatment.

Benefits

  • Access to affordable diagnosis and treatment of endocrine disorders.
  • Subsidized costs for consultation with endocrinologists and other specialists.
  • Free or subsidized medications for managing diabetes, thyroid disorders, and other hormonal imbalances.
  • Free regular screenings and check-ups for endocrine health.
  • Support for patients in need of surgeries or therapies related to endocrine conditions.
  • Financial assistance for expensive treatments like insulin therapy and hormone replacement therapy.

Eligibility

  • Residents of India with a diagnosis of any endocrine disorder (e.g., diabetes, thyroid disorders, hormonal imbalances).
  • Individuals who require specialized treatment and long-term management for endocrine conditions.
  • No income or caste-based restrictions apply for the scheme.
  • All age groups are eligible for treatment under the scheme, including children and elderly individuals.

Application Process

  • Visit a government hospital or healthcare center offering endocrine health services.
  • Register for the National Endocrine Health Scheme at the facility and provide necessary medical records.
  • Fill out the application form to avail of benefits for treatment and financial assistance.
  • Ensure regular follow-up visits as per the treatment plan and avail of free or subsidized medication as prescribed by the healthcare provider.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Medical certificate or diagnosis of an endocrine disorder (e.g., diabetes, thyroid conditions).
  • Income certificate (if applicable) to meet financial assistance criteria.
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, ration card, etc.).
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
