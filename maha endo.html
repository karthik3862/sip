Maharashtra Endocrine Disease Awareness Program

Scheme Details

The Maharashtra Endocrine Disease Awareness Program is designed to raise awareness about endocrine diseases, such as thyroid disorders, diabetes, and obesity. The program aims to educate the public on the causes, symptoms, prevention, and management of these diseases, as well as provide free screenings and consultations in government healthcare facilities.

Benefits

  • Free screening and consultations for endocrine diseases such as diabetes, thyroid disorders, and obesity.
  • Access to educational materials on prevention and management of endocrine diseases.
  • Free diagnostic tests for those at risk of endocrine diseases.
  • Referral to specialists for advanced treatment and care, if necessary.
  • Improved public awareness of endocrine health and disease prevention.
  • Access to counseling for lifestyle modification to manage or prevent endocrine disorders.

Eligibility

  • Residents of Maharashtra.
  • Individuals experiencing symptoms of endocrine diseases such as unexplained weight changes, fatigue, or mood swings.
  • Anyone interested in learning more about preventing or managing endocrine diseases.

Application Process

  • Visit the nearest government hospital or clinic offering the Maharashtra Endocrine Disease Awareness Program.
  • Register for the program and undergo a free screening to assess risk factors for endocrine diseases.
  • Consult with healthcare professionals for further guidance on managing or preventing these diseases.
  • Follow-up consultations and education on maintaining a healthy lifestyle and managing endocrine health.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, ration card, etc.).
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
