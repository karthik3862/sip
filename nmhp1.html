National Mental Health Programme

Scheme Details

The National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) aims to provide mental health care services to all individuals in need. It focuses on providing care to people with mental health issues, including services at the community level, and promoting mental health awareness across the country.

Benefits

  • Access to mental health care services, including counseling and psychiatric treatment.
  • Improved mental well-being through preventive and curative services.
  • Awareness and advocacy for mental health to reduce stigma.
  • Integration of mental health services into primary healthcare facilities.
  • Support for families and caregivers of people with mental health issues.
  • Training and capacity-building for mental health professionals.

Eligibility

  • Individuals who are experiencing mental health issues.
  • Residents of India who require mental health support.
  • Individuals in need of counseling, psychiatric treatment, or rehabilitation.
  • People seeking mental health awareness and education programs.

Application Process

  • Contact the nearest government hospital or primary health center that offers mental health services.
  • Fill out the application form to receive mental health counseling or treatment services.
  • Submit necessary documents for verification (if applicable).
  • Once your application is processed, you'll be assigned a mental health professional to assist you.
  • Access to services will be provided based on the type and severity of the mental health issue.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Medical history or diagnosis (if available).
  • Referral letter from a healthcare provider (if applicable).
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, ration card, etc.).
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
