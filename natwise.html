National Diabetes Prevention Program

Scheme Details

The National Diabetes Prevention Program (NDPP) is a national initiative aimed at preventing the onset of type 2 diabetes in high-risk individuals through lifestyle changes. The program provides access to evidence-based interventions, including dietary counseling, physical activity, and support for behavior change to help reduce the risk of developing diabetes.

Benefits

  • Access to lifestyle interventions to reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes.
  • Personalized guidance on nutrition, exercise, and weight management.
  • Support from trained coaches to help participants achieve behavior change.
  • Reduced risk of developing type 2 diabetes for those with prediabetes.
  • Free or subsidized program participation in government healthcare facilities.
  • Access to community-based support groups for ongoing motivation.

Eligibility

  • Adults aged 18 years and older with a high risk of developing type 2 diabetes.
  • Individuals with prediabetes or those diagnosed with risk factors for diabetes (such as obesity or a family history of diabetes).
  • Residents of India, with no income or caste-based restrictions to participate in the program.

Application Process

  • Visit a participating government healthcare center or clinic offering the National Diabetes Prevention Program.
  • Register for the program and undergo a health screening to assess your risk for diabetes.
  • Participate in the program which includes education on nutrition, exercise, and behavior change to reduce your risk of diabetes.
  • Follow-up visits and support will be provided to help maintain lifestyle changes and monitor health improvements.

Documents Required

  • Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, etc.).
  • Health records or documentation of risk factors (e.g., blood sugar levels, weight, or medical history).
  • Proof of residence (e.g., utility bill, ration card, etc.).
  • Recent passport-size photograph.
